# Rockchip RK3588

A popular, low-cost Arm SoC (found in Orange Pi 5, Radxa Rock 5, Khadas Edge2, and many others) with an integrated **6-TOPS NPU**. The sweet spot for "cheap Linux SBC that can still do on-device AI," with a fast-growing community around running small LLMs on the NPU.

- **Official toolchains:** [airockchip/rknn-toolkit2](https://github.com/airockchip/rknn-toolkit2) · [airockchip/rknn-llm](https://github.com/airockchip/rknn-llm)
- **Community hub:** [choushunn/awesome-RK3588](https://github.com/choushunn/awesome-RK3588)

## Specs that matter
- **CPU:** 8-core — 4× Cortex-A76 + 4× Cortex-A55.
- **NPU:** **6 TOPS (INT8)**, three cores (can run models in parallel or fuse for one big model).
- **GPU:** Mali-G610 (some OpenCL/vision use).
- **Memory/board:** varies widely by vendor board (4–32 GB LPDDR), which heavily affects what LLMs you can fit.

## Software
- **RKNN-Toolkit2** (v2.3.x) — convert ONNX/PyTorch/TFLite models to the `.rknn` format and quantize for the NPU.
- **RKNN Model Zoo** — ready examples (YOLO11, SAM, Whisper, CLIP, etc.).
- **RKNN-LLM (`rkllm`)** — run small LLMs (Qwen, Llama-class) on the NPU; the basis of community tools.
- **Community tools:** `Pelochus/ezrknpu` (easy NPU setup), `NotPunchnox/rkllama` (Ollama-like LLM server on RK3588).

## Strengths & trade-offs
- ✅ Very cheap (boards from ~$80); low power; widely available.
- ✅ Active community; rkllm makes on-device small-LLM experiments accessible.
- ⚠️ Only **6 TOPS** — fine for one or two CV streams, not heavy multi-camera analytics.
- ⚠️ Toolchain/quantization is less polished than NVIDIA/Intel; expect some debugging.
- ⚠️ Board quality and BSP/driver support vary a lot by vendor — pick a well-supported board.
